# Agile and Waterfall

Agile and Waterfall are delivery approaches. They describe how teams organise work, handle change, and move from idea to outcome.

## The Difference

<div class="de-article-compare">
  <article>
    <h3>Waterfall</h3>
    <p>Plan first, build in sequence, then test and deliver. It works best when requirements are stable, the outcome is clear, and late changes would be expensive.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Agile</h3>
    <p>Work in smaller cycles, learn as you go, and adapt based on feedback. It works best when requirements may change or when users need to shape the solution.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## What It Looks Like

<div class="de-data-flow">
  <span>Plan</span>
  <span>Build</span>
  <span>Test</span>
  <span>Review</span>
  <span>Improve</span>
</div>

Waterfall puts more weight on the early plan. Agile puts more weight on repeated feedback.

## When Each Can Help

<div class="de-callout-grid">
  <article>
    <h3>Use Waterfall thinking when...</h3>
    <p>The task has fixed requirements, clear approval stages, and little room for change.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Use Agile thinking when...</h3>
    <p>The team needs to test ideas, learn from users, and adjust direction as evidence appears.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Use judgement when...</h3>
    <p>The real world is mixed. Many teams use a planned structure with Agile habits inside it.</p>
  </article>
</div>
