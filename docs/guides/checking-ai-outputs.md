# Checking AI Outputs

AI tools can produce useful drafts, summaries, explanations, and ideas. They can also produce confident mistakes, missing context, weak reasoning, or content that is not safe to use.

Checking AI output is the skill of reviewing the answer before you rely on it, share it, or use it to make a decision.

## The Big Idea

AI output should be treated as a draft, not a final authority. The answer may be useful, but you are still responsible for checking whether it is accurate, appropriate, safe, and suitable for the situation.

This matters most when the output could affect people, decisions, learning, safety, money, data, or reputation.

<section class="de-key-term">
  <h2>Key Term</h2>
  <p><strong>Hallucination:</strong> an AI-generated statement that sounds plausible but is false, unsupported, or invented.</p>
</section>

## What To Check

<div class="de-check-card">
  <h2>Before using AI output, ask:</h2>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Is the answer factually accurate?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Does it match the original source or request?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Has it missed important context?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Is the tone suitable for the audience?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Are there assumptions that need checking?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Does it include personal, confidential, or sensitive information?</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox"> Could someone be harmed or misled if this is wrong?</label>
</div>

## Common Problems In AI Outputs

<div class="de-callout-grid">
  <article>
    <h3>Confident errors</h3>
    <p>The answer sounds certain but includes incorrect or invented details.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Missing context</h3>
    <p>The answer ignores important constraints, audience needs, or organisational rules.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Weak evidence</h3>
    <p>The answer makes claims without showing where they came from or how to verify them.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Wrong tone</h3>
    <p>The answer may be too casual, too formal, too blunt, or inappropriate for the reader.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Match The Check To The Risk

Not every AI output needs the same level of review. A draft title and a policy summary carry different levels of risk.

| Output | Check needed |
| --- | --- |
| Brainstormed ideas | Check usefulness, relevance, and tone. |
| Draft email | Check accuracy, audience, tone, and action requested. |
| Summary of notes | Compare against the original source. |
| Technical explanation | Check with trusted documentation or a knowledgeable person. |
| Policy, legal, financial, or personal advice | Treat as high risk and verify through approved sources. |

## Ask The AI To Help You Check

AI can help review its own output, but that does not replace your judgement. Use it to make the review easier.

```md
Review your previous answer. List:
1. Any assumptions you made.
2. Any claims that should be verified.
3. Any missing context that would improve the answer.
4. Any risks if this advice is followed without checking.
```

This prompt is useful because it asks the tool to expose uncertainty instead of polishing the answer.

## Use Trusted Sources

When accuracy matters, check the output against reliable sources. The right source depends on the task.

<div class="de-process-steps">
  <article>
    <span>1</span>
    <h3>Find the claim</h3>
    <p>Identify the statement that needs checking.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>2</span>
    <h3>Find the source</h3>
    <p>Use official documentation, internal guidance, source notes, or subject experts.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>3</span>
    <h3>Compare carefully</h3>
    <p>Check whether the AI changed meaning, missed conditions, or added unsupported detail.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>4</span>
    <h3>Update or reject</h3>
    <p>Correct the output, add caveats, or do not use it if the risk is too high.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Watch For Bias And Exclusion

AI outputs can reflect patterns in the data and examples they were trained on. This can affect language, assumptions, representation, and who the answer seems to include or exclude.

<details class="de-check-understanding" open>
  <summary>Bias check</summary>
  <ol>
    <li>Does the answer make assumptions about people, roles, backgrounds, or ability?</li>
    <li>Would the wording be appropriate for the full intended audience?</li>
    <li>Are any groups stereotyped, ignored, or described unfairly?</li>
    <li>Could the answer create barriers for someone using assistive technology or plain-English support?</li>
  </ol>
</details>
