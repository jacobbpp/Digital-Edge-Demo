# Strong And Weak AI Prompting

Prompting is the skill of asking an AI tool for help in a way that gives it enough context to produce a useful answer.

A strong prompt does not guarantee a perfect response, but it does make the response more likely to be relevant, usable, and easier to check. A weak prompt often creates vague, generic, or misleading output because the AI has to guess too much.

## The Big Idea

AI tools respond to the information they are given. If the request is unclear, missing context, or too broad, the answer may sound confident while still being unhelpful.

Good prompting is not about tricking the tool. It is about communicating the task clearly, setting boundaries, and making the output easier to review.

<section class="de-key-term">
  <h2>Key Term</h2>
  <p><strong>Prompt:</strong> the instruction, question, context, or source material you give to an AI tool so it can produce a response.</p>
</section>

## What Makes A Prompt Weak

Weak prompts usually make the AI guess the audience, purpose, format, level of detail, and success criteria.

<div class="de-article-compare">
  <article>
    <h3>Weak prompt</h3>
    <p>Explain APIs.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>Stronger prompt</h3>
    <p>Explain APIs to a non-technical apprentice in plain English. Use a workplace example, avoid jargon where possible, and finish with three knowledge-check questions.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Prompt Ingredients

| Ingredient | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Task | Summarise these meeting notes. |
| Context | This is for a learner support update. |
| Audience | The reader is a busy line manager. |
| Format | Return five bullet points and one action list. |
| Constraint | Keep the tone calm, professional, and plain English. |
| Quality bar | Highlight anything uncertain rather than inventing details. |

## Ask For The Right Shape

AI outputs are easier to use when you ask for a clear shape. This could be a table, checklist, summary, draft email, comparison, lesson outline, or list of risks.

<div class="de-callout-grid">
  <article>
    <h3>For planning</h3>
    <p>Ask for steps, dependencies, risks, and questions to clarify.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>For writing</h3>
    <p>Ask for audience, tone, structure, and a specific length.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>For learning</h3>
    <p>Ask for plain-English explanation, examples, misconceptions, and a knowledge check.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <h3>For review</h3>
    <p>Ask what is unclear, unsupported, too vague, or missing from the draft.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Improve The Prompt, Not Just The Answer

If the first answer is weak, do not only ask the AI to "try again". Improve the request.

<div class="de-process-steps">
  <article>
    <span>1</span>
    <h3>Spot the problem</h3>
    <p>Is the answer too vague, too long, too technical, too generic, or missing the audience?</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>2</span>
    <h3>Add context</h3>
    <p>Give the tool more information about the situation, user, source material, or goal.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>3</span>
    <h3>Set the format</h3>
    <p>Ask for the output in a structure that helps you use and check it.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>4</span>
    <h3>Request uncertainty</h3>
    <p>Ask it to identify assumptions, missing information, or areas that need checking.</p>
  </article>
</div>

## Use AI Safely

Do not paste learner data, employer information, customer details, internal documents, credentials, or confidential material into an AI tool unless your organisation has approved that use.

<details class="de-check-understanding" open>
  <summary>Safety check</summary>
  <ol>
    <li>Am I sharing personal, confidential, or sensitive information?</li>
    <li>Has this tool been approved for this type of work?</li>
    <li>Could the answer affect a learner, customer, colleague, or decision?</li>
    <li>What must I check before using or sharing the output?</li>
  </ol>
</details>
